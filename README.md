# Machine-Learning--Jaza-Energy-Project
The Jaza project aimed to evaluate and predict the potential success of Jaza Energy sites by integrating surveys, operational, and geospatial data. The primary objective was to validate currently used predictors of Jaza site success, refine and enrich site success criteria by retrospective analysis of GIS and Jaza survey data, and incorporate external survey data. Dalberg Research was expected to provide Jaza with the most powerful variables to predict Jaza success, a site selection model, an updated list and map of potential Jaza sites in Nigeria and Tanzania, and a recommendation on the site selection process. Further, Dalberg Research was to provide a summary report and documentation on the data from Jaza, Dalberg Research, and the site selection approach. 
Dalberg identified the strongest variables for predicting the success of Jaza sites using bivariate analysis, including correlation analysis of GIS parameters, survey indicators, and site performance outcomes. In addition, Dalberg Research used machine learning models for feature engineering and selection. The Machine Learning models were then trained using data from Tanzania and Nigeria, and evaluated using multiple performance metrics, to identify the most optimal model for predicting Jaza site success. 
The LOCAN team contributed to the predictive component of the project by providing a dataset containing geographic points across the entire Tanzania and Nigeria countries that represented potential candidate locations for future sites. The predictive methods used data from these points to estimate the likelihood of site success in those areas. In addition to the ML predictions, Dalberg Research used LOCAN predictive techniques, such as threshold-based models and GIS-based suitability mapping, using the top variables to further validate and operationalize the insights. The LOCAN team also provided contextual knowledge from the field, helped clarify how certain variables were collected, and validated whether the patterns identified in the analysis aligned with operational realities.
Finally, Dalberg Research synthesized the analytical findings into reports and presentations for project stakeholders, translating technical outputs into clear insights and highlighting the main drivers of site performance. The collaboration between the analytical modeling and LOCAN’s operational expertise strengthened the overall findings and helped ensure that the recommendations were both data-driven and practically applicable.
